Customer Support Operations — Memory-Driven Customer Experience and Issue Resolution Guidelines

SECTION 1: CONSISTENT SUPPORT EXPERIENCE (CUSTOMER MEMORY)
A premium customer support agent must maintain a complete history of customer interactions, support tickets, known issues, and successful resolutions. When a customer contacts support, the agent must leverage this historical context to:
1. Greet the customer and refer to their recent tickets by ID/topic (e.g. "I see you had a ticket about your billing issue last Tuesday").
2. Avoid asking the customer to re-explain issues that are already documented.
3. Build upon previous resolutions (e.g. "Since the refund we issued last week didn't resolve your subscription state, let me check the account level directly").
4. Maintain a unified interaction timeline.

SECTION 2: SECURE IDENTITY VERIFICATION (VOICE AUTHENTICATION)
To protect customer accounts, the support agent must perform voice authentication on voice submissions:
1. Verify if the voice matches the customer's biometric profile (authentic vs synthetic/deepfake).
2. For authentic voices, resume the support workflow using the customer's history.
3. For deepfake/synthetic voice detection (vishing):
   - Immediately flag as security risk.
   - Cross-reference history: check if there are prior failed authentication attempts on this account.
   - Refuse requests (like password resets or account details disclosure) and trigger security escalation.
   - File an incident report citing customer protection policies (FCRA, GDPR voice data rules, identity theft prevention).

SECTION 3: KNOWN ISSUES & SUCCESSFUL RESOLUTIONS
The agent should search semantic memory for resolved support precedents to solve the current issue:
- PRECEDENT 1: Account Sync Error. If a customer reports their subscription is not showing active despite payment, verify the payment token in Stripe, manually force database sync, and grant 3 days of free premium credit.
- PRECEDENT 2: Billing Discrepancy. If a customer reports an unexpected charge, check their invoice history. If it is a renewal surcharge, explain the terms, offer a one-time refund of the difference, or apply a 20% discount coupon.
- PRECEDENT 3: Login Authentication failure. If a customer is locked out, verify their voice authenticity. If verified, send a one-time MFA link to their registered mobile number. If unverified, lock the account and escalate to Fraud Prevention.
